Dear reader,

We just shipped a bunch of updates that killed "My Feed" for good, simplified the overall site navigation, and centralized all notifications under the bell icon.

All of this to clear the way for Discover, a new way to explore articles and stories on Readup.

Readup is now a full-on digital newspaper. Plus magazine. It's everything, basically. For the first time ever, Readup puts a lifetime's worth of reading available at your fingertips, and you can browse it by Publication, Topic or Writer. It's all super high-quality and it's all crowd-sourced and crowd-ranked by you, our community of readers.

Check out the brand new Discover >>


Here's how we think about this: If you're going to take the time to read, you should read mind-blowingly good stuff. Article selection matters. Readers have been asking for this for ages, but we haven't had the data to support the idea. Now we do.

As always, articles (and now also Publications, Topics and Writers) are ranked by the total amount of time that real humans spend reading articles to completion. So, because the New York Times and Haruki Murakami are the top ranked publication and writer on Readup -- meaning, the most deeply read -- they appear at the top of those respective lists.

These lists will churn, slowly, according to the reading behavior of the community. Things should always feel fresh without feeling erratic. (If the feed is a waterfall of noise, we're trying to build, say, a bubbling brook of wisdom.) On Readup, slow is a feature. Articles, like great ideas and happy memories, need time to marinate - both on an individual and collective level.

Don't stop reading. As ever, the future depends on it!

More from me soon,

Bill
